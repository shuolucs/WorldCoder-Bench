Solar Eclipse Info

A solar eclipse happens when the Moon passes between the Earth and the Sun, blocking some or all of the Sun’s light from reaching parts of Earth.

In this simulation, an eclipse is considered active when the Moon moves into a position approximately between Earth and the Sun. The scene lighting dims to show the effect of reduced sunlight, and the shadow intensity value estimates how strongly the eclipse affects Earth.

The anatomical reference model is included to support educational discussion about how living systems, including the nervous system, may respond to unusual environmental changes such as sudden darkness during an eclipse.
