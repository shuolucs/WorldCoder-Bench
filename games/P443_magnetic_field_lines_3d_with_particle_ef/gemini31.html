Magnetic Fields

Magnetic field lines represent the direction and strength of a magnetic field. They emerge from the North pole (red) and enter the South pole (blue).

Charged particles interact with these fields. In this simulation, positive charges (yellow) move along the field lines from North to South, while negative charges (cyan) move in the opposite direction.
