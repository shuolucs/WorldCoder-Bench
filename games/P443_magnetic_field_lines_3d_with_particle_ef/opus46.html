🧲 About Magnetic Fields

Magnetic field lines represent the direction and strength of a magnetic field. They emerge from the North pole (red) and curve around to enter the South pole (blue).

Charged particles moving through a magnetic field experience a force perpendicular to both their velocity and the field direction — this is the Lorentz force.

The closer the field lines are together, the stronger the magnetic field in that region. Try dragging the poles closer together to see this effect!
