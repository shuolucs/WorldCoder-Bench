Solar Eclipse Facts

  • A solar eclipse happens when the Moon moves between Earth and the Sun.
  • Total eclipses require very precise alignment of the three bodies.
  • The Moon’s umbra creates the darkest part of the shadow on Earth.
  • Because the Moon’s orbit is tilted, eclipses do not happen every month.
  • Observers in different locations may see total, partial, or no eclipse at all.
