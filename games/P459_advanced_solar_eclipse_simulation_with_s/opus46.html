🌑 Solar Eclipse Facts

  • A total solar eclipse occurs when the Moon completely covers the Sun's disk.
  • The Moon's shadow on Earth during a total eclipse is only about 100 miles wide.
  • Total solar eclipses can last up to 7.5 minutes, but most are shorter.
  • The Sun is about 400 times larger than the Moon, but also about 400 times farther away — creating the perfect alignment.
  • During totality, the Sun's corona becomes visible to the naked eye.
  • Solar eclipses have been recorded for over 4,000 years.
  • Animals often behave as if night has fallen during a total eclipse.
  • The temperature can drop by 10-15°F during totality.
