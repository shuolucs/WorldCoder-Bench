Solar Eclipse Facts

A solar eclipse occurs when the Moon passes between Earth and the Sun, thereby obscuring the view of the Sun from a small part of the Earth, totally or partially.

Umbra: The fully shaded inner region of a shadow cast by an opaque object. Experiencing the umbra means seeing a total eclipse.

Orbital Mechanics: The Moon's orbit is tilted by about 5 degrees relative to Earth's orbit around the Sun, which is why eclipses don't happen every month.
